How do planes fly?: There are four forces which are necessary for flight. These are lift, thrust, drag, and weight. These forces must be balanced to keep the plane in the air. You don’t need an engine to fly, as birds, paper planes, and gliders show us. In a plane, the engine doesn’t move the plane upward, it just moves it forward. The wings move it forward. For a plane to fly, the engines provide thrust which moves it forward through the air. As it moves, air flows rapidly over the wings. This creates a force called lift which keeps the plane in the air despite it being so heavy. Air moves more quickly over the top of the wing, creating an area of low pressure. Underneath the wing, air moves slowly, creating high pressure which lifts the wing upwards. This is known as Bernoulli’s Principle. Thrust comes from the engine and opposes drag, which is caused by air resistance. Lift comes from the change in pressure over the wing and opposes weight, which is caused by mass and gravity. It might sound quite complicated, but luckily pilots know what they are doing!
What is the Environmental impact of flying?: Everything that we do has an environmental impact. This means that they can contribute to climate change, which is the way that our behaviour has an effect on the overall climate. This is sometimes called a carbon footprint. Obviously, however, some things have a bigger impact than others. You probably already know that flying isn’t very good for the environment. Planes release a lot of carbon dioxide as a waste product from fuel. The aviation industry accounts for around 5% of global warming emissions, according to the BBC. That doesn’t sound like a lot, but consider that most people don’t fly very often and some people have never been on a plane in their whole life. Illustration of the earth with a fever and a thermometer sticking out of its mouth. You can reduce your carbon footprint by making some easy lifestyle changes. You could go on holiday in the UK instead of flying abroad. An easy change is to try walking or using public transport rather going in a car. You can also check the food miles on your groceries to make sure that they haven’t been flown from somewhere far away to get to you. Another good thing to do is to reduce the amount of plastic that you use and to recycle where possible.
AERONATICS: Aeronautics is the study of the science of flight. Aeronautics is the method of designing an airplane or other flying machine. There are four basic areas that aeronautical engineers must understand in order to be able to design planes. Aeronautics is the science or art involved with the study, design, and manufacturing of air flight-capable machines, and the techniques of operating aircraft and rockets within the atmosphere. The British Royal Aeronautical Society identifies the aspects of "aeronautical Art, Science and Engineering" and "The profession of Aeronautics (which expression includes Astronautics)."[1] While the term originally referred solely to operating the aircraft, it has since been expanded to include technology, business, and other aspects related to aircraft.[2] The term "aviation" is sometimes used interchangeably with aeronautics, although "aeronautics" includes lighter-than-air craft such as airships, and includes ballistic vehicles while "aviation" technically does not.[2] A significant part of aeronautical science is a branch of dynamics called aerodynamics, which deals with the motion of air and the way that it interacts with objects in motion, such as an aircraft.

You can make your flight even safer by following these guidelines: -Pay attention to the flight attendant safety briefing at the beginning of your flight and read the safety briefing card. -Buckle up.
